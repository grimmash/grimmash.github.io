Goal (Plex Order)</li><li>Margin</li></ul><div style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://grimmash.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/Chart-Day-15-300x2461.png" style="margin-left: 1em; center;">Daily Margin</div><div><br /></div></div><div><br /></div><div>The PLEXline is the lowest value of a PLEX that I can buy in the region I am in, plotted from $0 at day 0 and increasing each day by the PLEX value divided by 30, and multiplied by the day I am on in the month. &nbsp;That means on day 15, the PLEXline is 245m isk, based on a lowest PLEX value of 490m. &nbsp;This lets me see &nbsp;the pace at which I need to make isk to pay for the account, and how far above or below that amount I am on any given day.</div><div><br /></div><div>The PLEX Goal is just a horizontal gate I need to reach to give a relative idea of where I am overall.</div><div><br /></div><div>The Margin graph shows my actual gain each day relative to my starting isk amount.</div><div><br /></div><div>Fun fact: &nbsp;The buy order and cash line are mirror images of each other. &nbsp;This makes sense if you think about it for a while. &nbsp;They are simply two sides of the same pool of money varying based on how much I am investing into the market.</div><div><br /></div><div>An important note in all of this is that I track Liquid Isk separately from Ending Isk. &nbsp;I do this because Sell Orders have no real value, they are only the estimate of how much I stand to gain when I complete all the Sell Orders. &nbsp;Liquid Isk is what I could actually spend if I cancelled every Buy Order. &nbsp;I do not include assests or the real value of Sell Orders, as the real value of those would be the highest buy rates, and that's a lot of extra typing in Excel. &nbsp;I tracked the "real isk" amount for two days and stopped. &nbsp;Most of my margins sit in the 25% to 50% range, so I guess a useful estimate of real isk would be (and the graph):</div><div><br /></div><div>Buy
